Lifting the car to clear Engine and Cradle

Cranes

AKA Cherry Picker, etc.

When using a crane, watch length on lifting arm. Always set adjustable legs to maximum length.
Longer arm means less load arm can lift and can mean less stable when lifting high.
Many cranes are label but some don't have them or label has worn off.


Example from enginehoist.net

Most Crane's load rate as advertised is Maximum load and means shortest arm. Harbor Freight and others 1 ton crane is only rated 500Lb when arm is at maximum length. Too low for safe lifting a Fiero rear w/o cradle etc.

Trunk Latch/Strike

Never use the trunk lock strike has a lift point! The Strike is not made to take that much weight and you can bend, even destroy or tear out the strike.

Source: http://www.fiero.nl/forum/Forum2/HTML/088599.html

FUMP said: As many have said before, and have done before....used the trunk latch to lift their fieros up....Right now I am swapping out the engine of my 2.8L and guess what happened...as I was lifting the back end of my car up....BOOOM, the latch gave way (no rust) and the entire car came crashing down....This my friends could have easily killed someone.

Lesson learned...don't trust anything that holds up your car and be careful....

Source: http://www.fiero.nl/forum/Forum2/HTML/118167.html

Toddster did some math See "BIG TIP #7:" (Very good writeup for cradle removal.) and saying it should be OK but Do not trust the strike! Any small defect and the strike can fail without warning. GM never designed the trunk hardware to be a lifting/jacking point to work on the car.

  • Just bending the part should give a sign that the part is too weak.
  • The loop is not welded to the bracket. GM only swag the loop into the weak sheet metal.
  • The strike bracket is adjustable...

This is what the bracket look like when you remove the bolts. Notice the scratch ring and small dents when the factory tightens the left bolt. Depending on the adjustment, not much metal to hold major load. (Right bolt has same but hidden by the washer.)

Notice: Bolts are Class 9.8 and not "Grade 8" as Toddster said. Class 10.9 is about equal to Grade 8.

Source: 1986 Fiero

Trunk wall

Some says pull out part of the trunk carpet and use two holes in the reinforcement. This is not made as lifting point either. Metal is too thin and depending on hook in question, the hooks can pull out. Be very careful if going that plan. Example:

Source: http://www.fiero.nl/forum/Forum4/HTML/057576.html#p28

Bumper

Some says remove the rear fascia but this is a lot of effort for doing engine work... Works well using a crane. Notice the lifting arm is set to short length in this Example.

Take the bumper skin off. We then take a lifting strap rated for 6500 lbs, and run it under the bumper bar, between the bumper bar and the trunk, and attach the lift chain to both ends. If you have some of those nice weight rated locking loops, you can use them, or we just ran the chain through the open ends, and ran it back up to the middle lift hook.

Source: http://www.fiero.nl/forum/Forum2/HTML/118167.html#p29

Strut Towers

Works well when use with overhead hoist.

Better option w/o pulling body panel, etc. Use the Strut towers, which are much heavier metal that trunk reinforcement.

  1. Unbolt struts, cradle, etc, as normal.

  2. Use a jack to lift the car enough for the struts to clear the frame a few inches.

  3. Using Chain, Repair Links, and some Rebar or scrap steel to button hole the strut tower and lift car using strut towers.

Use tape or wire to keep the rebar center on chain.

Use long rebar as possible so rebar can't slip out if rebar isn't centered and spreads out load for added safety...

If you setup won't go thru the small hole then put chain and link thru tower then put rebar thru link underneath.

Jacking

Only on concrete pavment. Jack could shift/sink on soft surface.
Make sure the jack's wheels are free to move.

Using a Floor Jack and Long 4x4, just lift the car.

Source: http://www.fiero.nl/forum/Forum2/HTML/088599.html#p28
