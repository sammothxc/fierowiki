Fri, 08 Jan 1999
Dennis LaGrua, Neshanic, NJ
dlagrua@rcn.com

McKibben Sail Panels (QTR Windows) Evaluation Report

"If you own an '85-'88 Fiero notchback it is not a question of if you will need to replace your quarter windows or sail panels, it is a question of when. I own 4 Fieros and the panels are going on three of them. This is because of age from being exposed to extremes of humidity, heat, cold and other environmental factors. They become brittle and often crack. If you are lucky and they don't crack the paint will sometimes flake off the back and of course scratches are common. Car washes really don't help matters either, especially when alcohol based cleaners like Windex are used to clean them. Over the course of a few years, these parts end up looking plain cruddy. So what is the solution??? Paul McKibben of GA. has been manufacturing replacement Fiero sail panels for quite a few months now and I have heard only good things about them. Having acquired one of Paul's panels for evaluation I set out to judge the fit, finish and overall quality. I had the pleasure to evaluate a sail panel with a custom look. The panel is black with a Pegasus logo. However, for those desiring the exact OEM look, Paul also offers panels with SE, & GT logos in addition to clear ones, all black panels and a panel with the "Formula" script on it. So how good are these panels? In one word excellent. We checked the fit in the sail panel frame and the fit was perfect. The graphic image (logo) was sharp and clear and appeared to be heat stamped from the back of the panel. The shine and black color of the panel also matched the original Fiero panel perfectly. Having seen and evaluated Paul McKibben's sail panels first hand, it is easy to notice that the craftsmanship is top notch. Paul has apparently duplicated the original panels perfectly and they are very easy to install. In fact they look so much like the original OEM panels that I doubt if an expert show judge could tell the difference."

"OEM sail panels are now getting harder to find and are expensive. The panels with the GT logo are particularly hard to find. McKibben's sail panels now offer a solution. In short this is an outstanding product at a moderate price. Great job Paul. If there were a best new Fiero product of the year award these parts would certainly be in the running."

Dennis LaGrua, Neshanic, NJ
