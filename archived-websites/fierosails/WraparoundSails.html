Wraparound Sail Panels

The photos below show our prototype "wraparound" sail panel for the "notchback" style Fiero. (The "notchback" style is any Fiero other than '86-88 GT.) At the moment we are simply trying to gage interest in the style. Frankly, it's difficult to make this sail panel, there are many bugs yet to be overcome, installation involves a fair amount of work for the end user, and we don't want to attempt production without some indication of interest. This style is definitely not for everyone!

Take a look at the photos, read the installation requirements, and if you are interested in purchasing a set, please drop me a note at fierosails@yahoo.com. There is no obligation, naturally, since this is a "poll". Of course if you have suggestions or questions, they are always welcome.

Assuming the remaining bugs can be worked out, a pair of plain black wraparound sail panels would be $84. Graphics can be added at additional cost. The "Pegasus" in the sample would be $10 per panel. A standard "GT" or "SE" would be $5 per panel. One-of-a-kind graphics would have to be priced on an as-requested basis. Shipping would be around $5-$6 to the US and Canada, providing we can find a box that will fit properly. Double-sided tape is optional (lots of folks provide their own) at $3 per panel.     A few points to note:

    (1) The sail panel will be provided flat. The curvature is achieved by the purchaser during installation, hence the need for the clamps shown in the photo.

    (2) There is a lot of room on the sail panel (compared to the original) to accommodate graphics. This might be of interest to "project" cars or race vehicles.

    (3) The front edge of the sail panel must be heated to wrap around the front of the sail panel frame. There is a heat-affected area where this bend occurs and it is visible as a slight distortion in the acrylic. We will try to eliminate this in the final product, but it may be impossible. We tried to show this in the photo, but it won't show up.

    (4) Detailed instructions on modifying the sail panel frame and installing the sail panel will be posted on our web site (with photos) if production becomes a reality.

    (5) There is an interior vent at the base of the B pillar near the floor behind each seat. This communicates via the inside of the B pillar to the "vent" in the original sail panel frame. This affords some flow-thorough ventilation for the cabin. The wraparound sail panel will cover this vent and there may be some concern about adequate ventilation. Earl Sessions has used a "wraparound" sail panel for four years and reports no change in ventilation. A look at the photo below will show why. There is so much room for air to escape around the body panels that we suspect the "vent" in the original sail panel may have been for aesthetics.
